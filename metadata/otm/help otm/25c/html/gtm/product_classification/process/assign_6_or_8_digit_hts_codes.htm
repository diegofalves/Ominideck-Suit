Assign 6 or 8 Digit HTS Codes

In addition to the standard assignment of harmonized schedule 10-digit codes, GTM supports the assignment of 6- or 8-digit codes to items, trade transactions, declarations and item structures.

In some instances, you may want to assign a code other than the 10-digit code to business objects in GTM. For example, one of the items that you are importing is wrist watches. The watch consists of a movement, case, band, and battery. In the harmonized tariff schedule for the US, this is modeled as follows:

  • A watch with one jewel has a product classification code at the 8-digit level with the code being 9101.11.80. The parts that make up the watch all have product classification codes at the 10-digit level:
  • Movement has a code of 9101.11.4010
  • Case has a code of 9101.11.4020
  • Strap, band or bracelet has a code of 9101.11.4030
  • Battery has a code of 9101.11.4040

As part of your business process, your company may only assign the harmonized schedule 6-digit code to the watch and all of the components. In other instances, with a bill of material that is driven by the tariff, the 8-digit code needs to be assigned to an item. In the item structure your company may require the 10-digit codes at the component level.

To support the ability to assign codes at levels other than the 10-digit level, GTM needs to receive this data from the third party data provider in a specific format.
